# P1 exact diagnostic dry-fit checklist

`(CAD-01/P0-CAD)` · physical evidence form · **does not authorize production CAD or
select the right-deck architecture**.

## Preconditions and evidence discipline

- [ ] Mechanical floor is assembled to the ASM-05 state; DAT-F is clean and screw-head
  protrusion/flatness are photographed.
- [ ] Physical S0 is measured with the 2024 shell seated normally, not held up by a hand.
- [ ] Belt side is identified and recorded against P0 `L<0`; driver-left/right naming is
  recorded separately so it cannot change relative placement.
- [ ] D-27 occupancy is marked on a printed floor map: free, donor-occupied, shareable
  only with approval, or prohibited. Do not remove a donor screw merely to make a support fit.
- [ ] Steering is powered/centred as required, then swept lock-to-lock. Mark the physical
  D-26 rod/joint envelope; do not infer it from the diagnostic parts.
- [ ] Use the I.1 gauges: 5 mm static, 8 mm moving, 10 mm ESC-fan clearance.
- [ ] Photograph a scale/ruler and the relevant ID/arrow in every frame. Use file stem
  `P1_<item>_<view>_<pass-or-fail>.jpg`; take top, side and access/removal views.
- [ ] Record every failure as an observed overlap/gap in millimetres. Do not solve a
  failure by trimming a diagnostic part without first recording the original evidence.

For every row below, an empty box is an unperformed check. `N/A` is valid only when it is
written into the P1 record with a reason.

## Decision-control observations

These observations control later architecture work. Check a result only after the
referenced photos and measurements exist; this session does not decide any row.

| Controlled outcome | Required P1 observation | Pass/fail consequence to carry forward |
|---|---|---|
| Narrow right-deck continuation | Physical S0; shell clearance over CTL-E1/E2, amp and Wi-Fi maximum at H20; measured D-26 boundary; connector/USB/CN-16 access; insertion/removal path | Continue only if the inboard-hugged study can maintain I.1 clearances and service paths. This is evidence for a later CAD-05 decision, not CAD-05 authorization. |
| Fallback A activation | P2s/body-on clearance is below the H.4 trigger or the complete H20 dummy cluster cannot be installed/removed with policy clearance | Record fallback trigger as observed and return to the architecture decision process. Do not model fallback production geometry in P1. |
| Battery-tray revision | Any body/XT60/strap/bend/shell/KO-19/D-26/floor/clamp conflict; failure to remove pack top-only; lateral/F-R balance observation | Revise only the named tray parameter/feature, reprint plate 04 and repeat affected evidence. |
| UBEC-shelf revision | Either dummy fails to seat/lift; leads cannot reach without route violation; free feature is occupied; clamp fails; airflow/service/D-26 clearance fails | Change pocket, origin or attachment parameters; preserve separate Rail A/B and rerun affected P1 rows. D-24 may later force a distinct upsize revision. |
| Junction-support revision | Vent/body seat blocked; connectors/hand/tool/extraction fail; clamp/R2 conflict; body-on key hand path fails; DN blanks constrain decisions | Revise the open L-frame/seat/attachment/access parameter only; keep DN-01/DN-02 open and add no chassis hole. |
| Post-height selection | Caliper-confirmed H20/H26/H32 top plane, shell clearance, D-26 clearance, lower-layer lead access and dummy-cluster service result | Select the lowest passing diagnostic height for later review. Do not infer a deck outline or production post from height alone. |
